Phishing Information

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of online scam where criminals send fake emails pretending to be from legitimate companies to steal your personal information. They try to steal information such as:

  • Login Credentials (bank accounts, social media)
  • Credit card numbers
  • Social Security numbers
  • Other personal data

How Phishing Works

Scammers create emails that look like they're from real organizations (banks, government, etc.), they use different manipulation tactics, for example, saying that urgent action is required, to trick you into:

  • Clicking malicious links
  • Downloading infected attachments
  • Entering login credentials on fake websites

Example of a Phishing Email

Here's what a typical phishing email might look like:

From: Amazon Customer Service <support@amaz0n-update.com>
Subject: URGENT: Your account will be suspended

Dear Valued Customer,

We've detected unusual activity on your Amazon account. To prevent suspension, please verify your account immediately:

Verify Account Now

If you don't take action within 24 hours, your account will be permanently closed.

Amazon Customer Service Team

Red Flags in This Email:

  1. Suspicious sender address: "amaz0n-update.com" instead of "amazon.com"
  2. Urgent/threatening language: Creates false urgency to bypass your caution
  3. Generic greeting: Uses "Valued Customer" instead of your name
  4. Requests sensitive info: Legitimate companies never ask for credentials via email

How to Protect Yourself

It is good practice to never share sensitive info via email, legitimate companies wouldn't ask for passwords, SSN's, or credit card information in this manner.

You should always look for misspellings or strange domains (@amaz0n.com instead of @amazon.com). Before clicking on any link you should always hover over it to check the actual URL of the link.

If you are unsure then you should contact the organization directly using their official phone number, website, or email.

You can install tools on your devices that can detect and block malicious links or warn you so you don't fall victim to one of these attacks. Reporting these phishing emails to the impersonated company can help save other people from falling to the same scam.

Safety Tips

  • Always check the sender's email address carefully - not just the display name.
  • You should never have the same password across multiple sites, if one account is hacked, all accounts can be hacked too.

    • What to Do If You Fell for a Phishing Scam

    If you suspect you've responded to a phishing email or entered information on a fake website, take these immediate actions:

    1. Change Compromised Passwords

    • Immediately change the password for any account you entered credentials for
    • Use a new, strong password that you haven't used elsewhere
    • Enable two-factor authentication if available

    2. Financial Accounts

    • Contact your bank or credit card company if you entered financial information
    • Monitor accounts for unauthorized transactions
    • Request new cards if credit card numbers were compromised

    3. Scan for Malware

    • Run antivirus scans if you downloaded attachments or clicked links
    • Update your security software and operating system
    • Consider wiping and restoring your device if serious infection is suspected

    4. Report the Phishing

    • Forward the phishing email to reportphishing@apwg.org (Anti-Phishing Working Group)
    • Report to the impersonated organization (e.g., forward to reportphish@amazon.com if pretending to be Amazon)
    • File a report with the FTC at ReportFraud.ftc.gov

    5. Identity Theft Protection

    • Place a fraud alert with credit bureaus if personal information was shared
    • Consider freezing your credit to prevent new accounts being opened
    • Monitor your credit reports for suspicious activity
